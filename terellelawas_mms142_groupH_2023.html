How 3D Printing is a Disruptive Technology

What is Disruptive Technology?

Disruptive technology is a new technology that may not be as polished at first compared to the existing technologies but still has the potential to be fully developed.

Not all cases of disruptive technologies are harmful. The term “disruptive” in disruptive technology isn’t always a bad thing as it opens a lot of opportunities for different sectors at a lower and more accessible cost. Just like in the industry of 3D Printing, it does not exactly disrupt existing technologies but it raises the competition (Rayna and Striukova, 2015).

Innovation of Materials (Accessible and Affordable Manufacturing) (Terelle)

One of the most accessible and affordable materials that can be used in 3D Printing is food. Made by a team of engineers in Columbia University, cheesecake was made through 3D printing using only 7 food ingredients such as graham crackers liquified as paste, peanut butter, strawberry jam, Nutella, banana purée, cherry juice, and frosting. These 7 ingredients were used as “inks” to print one serving of cheesecake. They used a modified 3D printer called Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) which is a device that uses lasers to thermally process the ingredients in order for its output to be stable and have a proper texture and structure.

The engineers believe that patients with special medical needs and dietary restrictions will benefit from this 3D Printing innovation.
